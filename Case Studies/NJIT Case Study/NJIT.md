## üö® Overview & The Challenge
In the late 2010s, the **New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT)** faced a critical infrastructure challenge: its legacy Learning Management System (Moodle) was outdated, creating friction for end-users and bottlenecking the institution's ability to scale modern digital learning.
<br><br/>
### The Original Business Goal
Execute a phased selection, UAT pilot, and implementation of a modern, cloud-native SaaS LMS to future-proof the university's technical + digital learning infrastructure.
<br><br/>
### The Black Swan Event (Amended Goal)
Towards the end of our phased rollout, the sudden onset of the COVID-19 pandemic forced a global lockdown. The mandate shifted overnight: accelerate the adoption pipeline immediately and execute a complete transition from in-person to 100% remote learning environments to ensure business and educational continuity.
<br><br/>
### My Roles
* Instructional Designer
* SaaS Migration & Pilot Lead
* LMS System Administrator
<br><br/>
<br><br/>

## üèóÔ∏è Phase 1: Pre-Incident Architecture & Proactive Migration
As the LMS Pilot Lead, my approach was rooted in a methodical, data-driven migration strategy. Long before the crisis hit, my focus was on User Acceptance Testing (UAT) and building a scalable foundation.
<br><br/>
### Data-Driven Architecture Selection
I researched and analyzed market share data, adoption velocity, and System Usability Scale (SUS) scores across higher-education institutions to build a strong, evidence-based business case for the transition to Canvas.
<br><br/>
### Proactive Data Migration
Over several months, I partnered with one other Instructional Designer to manually migrate and QA the entire catalog of university course offerings from the legacy Moodle servers into Canvas. By the time the pandemic hit, we had successfully provisioned **~99% of the enterprise nodes**, ensuring the new infrastructure was fully loaded and ready for immediate deployment.
<br><br/>
### Enablement Roadmap
Anticipating the need for scalable onboarding, I designed and deployed self-paced enablement tutorials for both faculty (creators) and students (end-users). This self-serve documentation acted as a critical buffer, drastically reducing support tickets during the transition.

## ‚ö° Phase 2: Incident Response & Rapid Scaling (The Pandemic Pivot)
With the university's swift move to a 100% remote operating model, my role shifted from migration strategy to front-line **Disaster Recovery and Incident Response**. 
<br><br/>
### Accelerated Adoption Pipeline
I provided real-time enablement, consultation, and virtual training sprints, allowing over 300 faculty stakeholders to adopt the new SaaS platform and adapt their instructional design for synchronous virtual delivery in a matter of days (and continued support thereafter).
<br><br/>
### Environment Management & LTI Integration
Unlike our legacy open-source system, the new SaaS LMS infrastructure provided dedicated `Test` and `Beta` environments. I utilized these sandbox servers to safely QA custom configurations and securely provision third-party LTI (Learning Tools Interoperability) protocols via the App Center. Once validated in staging, I pushed these critical integrations to the production environment to support the remote ecosystem:
  * **Turnitin:** Plagiarism detection and academic integrity logic.
  * **Webex:** Synchronous virtual lecturing and remote communication infrastructure.
  * **ProctorU & Respondus:** Secure, remote exam proctoring environments.
<br><br/>
### Enterprise SaaS Administration
I took on the LMS Administrator role, actively managing the backend catalog of the university's course offerings, routing user permissions, and troubleshooting technical blockers in real-time to maintain system uptime.
<br><br/>
